Given a root subsystem \(P\), and a root sub-subsystem \({P}_{0}\), in (10.2) of Semisimple subalgebras of semisimple Lie algebras, E. Dynkin defines a numerical constant \(e{}\left(P, {P}_{0}\right)\) (which we call Dynkin epsilon).
In Theorem 10.3, Dynkin proves that if an \(sl{}\left(2\right)\) is an \(S\)-subalgebra in the root subalgebra generated by \(P\), such that it has characteristic 2 for all simple roots of \(P\) lying in \({P}_{0}\), then \(e{}\left(P, {P}_{0}\right)=0\). It turns out by direct computation that, in the current case of A71A^{1}_7, \(e \left(P, {P}_{0}\right)=0\) implies that an S-\(sl{}\left(2\right)\) subalgebra of the root subalgebra generated by P with characteristic with 2's in the simple roots of \({P}_{0}\) always exists. Note that Theorem 10.3 is stated in one direction only.
