In Theorem 10.3, Dynkin proves that if an sl(2) is an S-subalgebra in the root subalgebra generated by P, such that it has characteristic 2 for all simple roots of P lying in P_0, then e(P, P_0)= 0. It turns out by direct computation that, in the current case of C^{1}_6, e(P,P_0)= 0 implies that an S-sl(2) subalgebra of the root subalgebra generated by P with characteristic with 2's in the simple roots of P_0 always exists. Note that Theorem 10.3 is stated in one direction only.
